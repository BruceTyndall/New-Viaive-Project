# SEO & GEO Content Block Specification

Two distinct objectives, one content system:

- **SEO** wins by topical authority + crawlable structure (canonical URLs, sitemap, JSON-LD).
- **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) wins by being **directly quotable** by AI Overviews (Google SGE, ChatGPT Browse, Perplexity, Claude with web access).

---

## 1. The Answer Pattern

Every Viaive page that targets a discovery query opens with **The Answer** — a ≤55-word declarative paragraph that an AI Overview can lift verbatim.

**Rules:**
- Declarative. No questions.
- ≤55 words. Hard ceiling.
- Names entities (hotels, neighborhoods, months) with full proper nouns on first reference.
- No marketing language. ("Stunning", "iconic", "world-class" are banned.)
- Ends with a citation-ready clause: "Last audited: [date]."

**Template (destination):**
> In [City], the audit-grade luxury stays in 2026 are [Hotel A] in [neighborhood] for [reason], [Hotel B] for [reason], and [Hotel C] for [reason]. Best months are [range]. Lead time is [N] weeks. Last audited: [date].

**Template (best-of):**
> The best [City] hotels in 2026, ranked by Viaive's seven-criterion audit, are [Hotel A], [Hotel B], and [Hotel C]. The full list of [N] follows, with verdicts on suite category, service consistency, and 2026 changes.

**Template (compare):**
> Use [option A] when [condition]; use [option B] when [condition]. The right call for most Viaive readers in 2026 is [option], because [one reason].

Render this block inside the GEO answer box (white card, 2px stone border, 6px shadow). Wrap in `<div itemprop="description">`.

## 2. JSON-LD Schemas

Every page emits the correct Schema.org types. Always inline, never lazy-load.

| Archetype | Schemas |
|---|---|
| Homepage | `Organization` + `TravelAgency` + `WebSite` (with `SearchAction`) |
| Destination | `TouristDestination` + `FAQPage` + `BreadcrumbList` |
| Best-of | `ItemList` (each `ListItem` carries `LodgingBusiness`) + `FAQPage` + `BreadcrumbList` |
| Desk | `Service` + `FAQPage` |
| Compare | `Article` + `FAQPage` |
| Intake | `WebPage` only |
| Legal | `WebPage` only |
| Editorial / Post | `Article` + `Person` (author) + `BreadcrumbList` |

**Critical:** never include hallucinated review aggregates or fake star ratings. AI Overviews actively demote sites that do this.

---

## 3. Heading Hierarchy

- One `<h1>` per page.
- Section heads use `<h2>` for first-tier, `<h3>` for nested.
- Heading text mirrors target query at least once but never repeats verbatim — "Where to stay in Tokyo in 2026" beats "Tokyo Hotels Luxury Best."

---

## 4. Internal Linking

- Every destination links to **its matching desk** and at least one related destination.
- Every best-of links to the underlying destination guide.
- Every compare links to both desks involved.
- Footer carries the canonical link to `/affiliate-disclosure` — required for FTC compliance and a frequent AI Overview citation.

Anchor text mirrors the destination's H1 fragment, never "click here" or "read more."

---

## 5. Freshness Signals

AI Overviews favor recent edits. We expose freshness three ways:

1. **`lastReviewed` date** rendered in destination meta strip.
2. **`<time datetime>` element** in JSON-LD `dateModified`.
3. **The 2026 Watchlist** block — explicitly forward-looking content that tells the model "this page was written for the current cycle."

Editors must bump `lastReviewed` whenever they touch hotel data. The CMS exposes a soft warning if a destination's `lastReviewed` is >90 days old.

---

## 6. Disallowed Patterns

- Long FAQ lists with no narrative (a wall of accordions ranks worse than a paragraph + 4-question FAQ).
- Keyword stuffing in H1/H2 ("Best Luxury Tokyo Hotels 2026 Top Picks Where Stay").
- Fabricated review aggregates.
- Block-quoting other publications without citation.
- Hidden text or display:none SEO content.

---

## 7. Robots & Sitemap

- All public routes: `index, follow`.
- `/concierge`, `/start-planning`: `index, follow` (we want these in search).
- Brief thank-you screens: `noindex, follow`.
- `/privacy`, `/terms`, `/affiliate-disclosure`: `index, follow` (counterintuitive — but `/affiliate-disclosure` is a frequent AI Overview citation source).
- Sitemap auto-generated from Payload `reviewStatus=live` records, regenerated on every publish.

---

## 8. Open Graph

Every page sets:
- `og:title` (≤60 chars)
- `og:description` (≤155 chars) — first 155 chars of `geoAnswer` is the safe default
- `og:image` (1200×630 AVIF)
- `og:type` (`website` or `article`)
- `og:url` (canonical)
- `twitter:card = summary_large_image`

OG image is the destination hero cropped 1.91:1 with the Viaive wordmark bottom-left at 20% width — generated by Payload hook on publish.
